The Good: Capital Structure Is the Strongest in a Decade

Unrestricted net assets grew 140% since FY2018 ($1.8B→$4.3B). Debt-to-cap at 28.5% is well below the 33% A-rated ceiling. Pension virtually eliminated ($629M→$14M).

The Concern: Liquidity and Cash Generation Lag

Days cash stuck at ~122 vs. 225-day A-rated median. Operating CF margin at 2.0% vs. 7% benchmark. CapEx/depreciation at 8% = severe deferred maintenance. The balance sheet is improving despite operations, not because of them.

The Risk: Growth Driven by Investment Returns, Not Operations

Cumulative operating income FY2018–FY2025: $1.3B. But net assets grew $2.5B. The difference = investment market gains. In FY2022, investment losses of -$271M erased all operating surplus.

The Opportunity: Debt Capacity Headroom

At 28.5% debt-to-cap vs. 33% ceiling, MedStar has $300–400M in unused strategic debt capacity to fund transformation without credit deterioration.

Source: Audited Financial Statements, FY2018–FY2025  |  Benchmarks: Moody's A-rated health system medians

Capital Reinvestment Crisis: CapEx Has Never Exceeded 31% of Depreciation

Estimated $1.4B in Accumulated Deferred Maintenance (FY2018–FY2025)

With $2.3B in net PP&E and $272M in annual depreciation, the physical plant is aging without reinvestment. CapEx actually declined from $70M (FY2023) to $20M (FY2025) while revenue grew 16%. This is a slow-building crisis that eventually becomes emergency capital need.

Self-Insurance Reserves Are Growing: A Hidden Cash Drain

$394M in Reserves, Up 39% Since FY2018

Professional & general liability accruals: $448M (FY2025). Per-claim retention rose from $20M to $25M. Rising risk exposure in the malpractice-heavy MD/DC corridor.

Why It Matters

Self-insurance is a long-tail liability. Today's reserves fund yesterday's claims. If actuarial estimates prove inadequate, future cash calls accelerate — consuming operating cash flow that's already thin.

Source: Audited Financial Statements, FY2018–FY2025 + Note 11 (Self-Insurance Programs)
