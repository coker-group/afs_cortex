MedStar Health — Executive Summary 3 / 13

Where MedStar Is Winning

  • Revenue growth engine intact — 7.0% CAGR (FY2018–FY2025), accelerating to 8.7% in FY2025. Net patient service revenue grew from $4.7B to $7.5B.
  • Pension liability virtually eliminated — From $629M (FY2020) to $14M (FY2025), a $615M reduction. Plan is now overfunded with $58.7M in pension assets. Frees future cash flow.
  • Balance sheet dramatically strengthened — Unrestricted net assets grew from $1.8B to $4.3B (+140%). Debt-to-capitalization improved from 45.9% to 28.5%, well below A-rated ceiling of 33%.
  • Investment portfolio at $2.1B — Long-term investments nearly doubled since FY2018, providing strategic optionality and non-operating income.
  • Labor cost discipline — Labor as % of revenue improved from 57.7% peak (FY2020) to 54.3% (FY2025), now within the 50–55% benchmark range.

The Core Challenge: Growth Without Operating Leverage

Revenue and expenses are growing in lockstep — every dollar of new revenue is consumed by a matching dollar of cost

Revenue CAGR: 7.0%  |  OpEx CAGR: 7.0%  |  FY2018–FY2025. At $9B, each 1% of margin improvement = $90M.

  • Operating margin has never reached A-rated median (3.0–4.0%) — Best year was FY2021 at 3.86%, distorted by $200M+ in CARES Act revenues. Normalized margin is 1.9–2.4%.
  • EBIDA margin at 5.5% vs. 8–10% peer median — The 250–450bp gap on $9B revenue represents $225M+ in structural underperformance.
  • Operating CF margin collapsed to 2.0% — Down from 4.8% in FY2019. Healthy systems sustain 6–8%. MedStar generates ~$180M vs. a $540–720M benchmark.
  • FY2022 exposed the fragility — When stimulus evaporated, operating income fell to $81M on $7.3B revenue (1.1% margin). No buffer exists for the next cost shock.

What the Footnotes Reveal

Government Payer Concentration: 50%

Medicare (33%) + Medicaid (17%) = half of revenue from administratively-set rates. Limits pricing power and creates reimbursement risk from CMS policy changes.

Commercial AR Mismatch

Other commercial payers: 26% of revenue but 37% of AR ($388M). Suggests denial friction and slower collection — primary revenue cycle target.

Georgetown Obligations: ~$40M/year

Shared services ($20.5M), gain-sharing ($5M), conditional pledges ($15.1M). Fixed obligations regardless of operating performance. Affiliation extends to 2066.

Self-Insurance Rising: $448M Accrued

Professional liability retention up from $20M→$25M per claim. Workers' comp + employee health add $66M. Rising reserves signal increasing risk exposure in the MD/DC corridor.
