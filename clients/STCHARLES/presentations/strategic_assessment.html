Structural Labor: 58.3% of Revenue

Post-pandemic normalization stalled at 58%, well above the 50–55% A-rated median. Employee benefits grew 84% ($89M to $164M) versus 65% revenue growth, indicating healthcare cost inflation for SCHS' own workforce is outrunning revenue growth.

Pattern Deferred Maintenance Cycle

FY2020–FY2023: Capex/D&A collapsed to 44–73%, meaning the system spent less than half what was needed to maintain facilities. PP&E shrank from $434M to $376M. FY2024–FY2025: Massive $173M catch-up surge, driving capex/D&A to 134% then 271%.

Boom-bust capital cycles are costly — deferred work costs more when eventually done. Recommend a normalized multi-year capital plan.
Operating Cash Flow ($M) & CF Margin
Recovering from FY2021–FY2022 crisis but declining

Warning CF Ops Margin Declining

Operating CF margin fell from 7.4% (FY2024) to 4.9% (FY2025), below the A-rated target of 6–8%. The FY2021–FY2022 crisis ($84M combined negative CF) was driven by Medicare advance repayments. Those are resolved, but the current trajectory is concerning.

Strength Free Cash Flow Positive (Ex-Catch-Up)

In normalized years (FY2019, FY2023, FY2024), SCHS generates $20–60M in free cash flow. The FY2025 negative FCF (-$46M) reflects the extraordinary $118M capital program, not operating weakness.

Structural Risks & Concerns
The core issues that create the consulting opportunity

Critical Revenue-Expense Scissors

Expenses growing 70bps/year faster than revenue. Operating margin declined from 4.1% (FY2019) to 0.3% (FY2025). At this trajectory, the margin turns permanently negative within 2–3 years. This is an existential structural issue, not a cyclical blip.

Revenue CAGR: 9.8% | Expense CAGR: 10.5% | FY2025 op income: $4.4M on $1.45B

Critical Supply Cost Explosion

"Medical supplies, drugs, and other" doubled from $238M to $475M (12.2% CAGR vs. 9.8% revenue CAGR). At 32.8% of revenue, this is nearly double the typical benchmark. Even if definitional differences explain some of the gap, the 600bps deterioration since FY2019 is unambiguous.

$475M in FY2025 | 32.8% of revenue | +600bps since FY2019

Structural Labor Cost Overhang

At 58.3% of revenue, labor costs are 330bps above the A-rated ceiling. The FY2023 temporary improvement to 54.8% proved unsustainable. Benefits alone ($164M) grew 84% on 65% revenue growth — a compounding problem independent of wages.

Benefits load: 24% of salaries | CAGR: 10.7% | Target: 50–55% of revenue

Warning Non-Operating Income Dependency

FY2025: $142M total excess over expenses, but only $4.4M from operations. The $138M gap was investment returns ($108M) and acquisition gain ($25M). A 15% market downturn would wipe out the equivalent of 2+ years of operating income.

97% of total margin from non-operating sources in FY2025

Pattern Capital Boom-Bust Cycle

Four years of underinvestment (capex/D&A 44–73%) followed by a massive catch-up ($173M over FY2024–25). This pattern is costly — deferred work done under compressed timelines increases costs by 15–30% versus planned maintenance cycles.

PP&E shrank from $434M to $376M before recovering to $495M

Rising Self-Insurance & Litigation Exposure

IBNR liabilities grew 28% in one year ($10M to $12.85M), outpacing revenue growth. The $1M per-claim retention with a $7M aggregate cap means a few large claims could breach the buffer. Rising accruals signal increasing claims frequency or severity.
